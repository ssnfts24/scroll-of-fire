# 2. Earth’s Natural Electromagnetic Environment

## 2.1 Global Electrical Circuit (GEC)

The Global Electric Circuit describes the continuous flow of electrical current between Earth’s surface and the ionosphere.

Key scientific components:

- **Ionosphere**: Highly conductive plasma layer  
- **Earth’s surface**: Conductive lower boundary  
- **Atmosphere**: Weakly conducting dielectric  
- **Lightning**: Recharges ionospheric potential (~250–300 kV)  
- **Fair-weather current density**: ~1–3 pA/m² globally  

The GEC is foundational to atmospheric electricity research and is modeled in planetary electrodynamics.

## 2.2 Schumann Resonances

Schumann resonances are standing electromagnetic waves trapped in the Earth-ionosphere cavity.

They are:

- measurable  
- predictable  
- globally coherent  
- driven primarily by lightning activity  

Fundamental modes:

- 7.83 Hz  
- 14 Hz  
- 20 Hz  
- 26 Hz  
- 33 Hz  

These resonances are not metaphysical; they are **natural EM phenomena** well documented in atmospheric physics.

## 2.3 Geomagnetic Field & Wave Propagation

Earth’s magnetosphere guides the movement of charged particles and several classes of EM waves.

Important physical processes include:

- **Alfvén waves** — magnetohydrodynamic oscillations  
- **Whistler waves** — lightning-induced high-frequency propagation  
- **Plasmaspheric resonance** — field-line oscillations  
- **Solar wind–magnetosphere coupling**  
- **TEC (Total Electron Content) variations** — critical for GPS accuracy  

These processes create natural EM “channels,” but they do not exhibit consciousness or intention.
