T7 Field Practice

Suggested start: 432 Hz, sine, single tone, 7.83 Hz pulse, low volume. Use this as physical alignment, breath, balance, and field-awareness practice.

1 · Ground

Feel a slow pulse beneath the body. Let the breath settle first.

2 · Axis

Picture the spine as the central line of the field.

3 · Torus

Watch the field move outward, around, and back through center.

4 · Phase

Let breath and sound align without forcing.

5 · Witness

Notice one real body signal, one thought pattern, one shift.

6 · Seal

Stop cleanly, breathe normal, and log the witness.

Physical Lift Training · 7-Step Loop

Physical Lift Training

7-Step Lift Loop

Physical alignment practice for posture, breath, balance, nervous-system steadiness, and felt lightness. It trains the body to organize itself with less drag.

1 2 3 4 5 6 7

1 · Grounding Resonance

Feet planted. Slow pulse beneath feet and spine. Settle into support.

2 · Flow Medium

Picture air as layered currents. Notice pressure, warmth, and space.

3 · Body as Resonator

Spine as axis. Inhale up. Exhale down. Smooth jaw, shoulders, ribs.

4 · Phase Lock

Watch breath and posture line up. Notice lighter, steadier balance.

5 · Drift Feedback

Tension is data. Find whether drift began in breath, posture, thought, or effort.

6 · Lift Command

Use the cue: “brighten the center.” Let the body lengthen upward without strain.

7 · Witness & Integration

Pause, breathe normal, record balance, posture, body sensation, emotion, and clarity.

Safety · Ground Rules

Safety

Ground Rules

Keep volume low. Do not use while driving, operating tools, or doing anything that needs full attention. Stop if discomfort increases. Sudden severe headache, vision changes, weakness, confusion, seizure symptoms, or headache after injury should be medically evaluated.

Volume Rule

Start low. Avoid blasting tones, especially with headphones.

Attention Rule

Use only when seated, safe, and free from tasks that need full awareness.

Stop Rule

Pause immediately if you feel worse, dizzy, panicked, or disoriented.
