Physical Lift Training

7-Step Lift Loop

This is a physical alignment practice for posture, breath, balance, nervous-system steadiness, and felt lightness. It trains the body to organize itself with less drag.

1 2 3 4 5 6 7

1 · Grounding Resonance

Stand or sit with feet planted. Feel a slow pulse beneath the feet and spine. Let the body settle into support.

2 · Flow Medium

Picture the air as layered currents around the body. Notice space, pressure, warmth, and subtle movement.

3 · Body as Resonator

Use the spine as the central axis. Inhale up the axis. Exhale down the axis. Smooth the jaw, shoulders, and ribs.

4 · Phase Lock

Watch breath rhythm and body rhythm line up. Look for the moment where posture feels lighter and steadier.

5 · Drift Feedback

If tension returns, treat it as data. Notice whether drift began in breath, thought, posture, or effort.

6 · Lift Command

Use a simple cue: “brighten the center.” Let the body lengthen upward without strain or pushing.

7 · Witness & Integration

Pause before repeating. Record breath, balance, posture, body sensation, emotion, and clarity. The witness completes the loop and turns the exercise into memory.

Ground Rules
