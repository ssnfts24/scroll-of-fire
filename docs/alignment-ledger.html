Alignment Ledger

The Alignment Ledger records how moving astronomical gates meet the fixed Pattern Calendar from year to year. It measures recurrence and divergence without assuming that repetition proves cause or destiny.

Measurement methods

Each annual record identifies the March equinox UTC instant, its Pattern Calendar position, the Year Gate (Moon 1 · Day 1 = April 17 of each year), the Passage duration between them, and the approximate lunar phase at the equinox. Offsets are direct measurements. Symbolic layers (Week Gate, Archetype) are clearly labelled. Recurrence scores expose every component dimension; no unexplained index is produced.

Source and precision disclosure

Equinox instants are whole-second UTC timestamps imported from a third-party source (janmr.com) and independently validated against bazica. Maximum measured deviation: 2.057 seconds (2022). Lunar phase is approximate synodic calculation (known new moon 2000-01-06 18:14 UTC, period 29.530588853 days). Passage duration precision is limited by equinox timestamp resolution. Pattern Calendar positions are deterministic. Year Gate is defined as April 17 00:00 UTC each year. No claim is made that any measurement proves causation or destiny.
