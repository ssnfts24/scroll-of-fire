Part II · Observation

Witness

Record the event before the explanation becomes the event.

Witness is the discipline of preserving what was perceived, when it was perceived, under what conditions, and by whom. It does not eliminate interpretation. It prevents interpretation from silently replacing the record.

W

A witness record is strongest when it distinguishes direct observation, memory, interpretation, inference, and claim.

Plain Language

What Witness Means

Direct observation is not interpretation

Witness begins with what was directly seen, heard, felt, measured, or recorded before any theory explains it.

Timing and conditions matter

A record must retain when the event happened and what conditions shaped the observation at that time.

Who observes shapes what is seen

The witness is part of the field, so perspective, location, tools, and attention must be named rather than assumed away.

Witness records support revision

Preserved records make later correction possible because the original event remains available beside newer explanations.

Practice

The Witness Protocol

1 · Name the time and place

Locate the event before drawing any meaning from it.

2 · Name what was directly perceived

Record only what entered perception, measurement, or immediate trace.

3 · Separate memory from fresh perception

Keep prior knowledge and current observation in distinct entries.

4 · Name interpretations as interpretations

Inference may follow, but it must stay labeled as inference.

5 · Preserve the record unchanged

Append correction later instead of overwriting the original witness entry.

Key Takeaway

Keep the event and the explanation distinct.

A witness record cannot be revised by the explanation that came later. The event and its interpretation remain separate entries.
