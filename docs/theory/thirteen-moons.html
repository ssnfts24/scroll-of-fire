13 Moons Theory

Time becomes a mirror when witness returns at a consistent rhythm.

The 13 Moons system is a symbolic calendar architecture built around thirteen equal 28-day cycles. Its purpose is not to replace civil time or astronomical measurement. It gives the Codex a repeating frame for daily witness, pattern review, memory, seasonal transition, and return.

13×28

The visible lunar phase is an astronomical observation. The 28-day Moon structure is the Codex’s regular symbolic and organizational cycle.

Orientation

Three layers of time remain distinct.

Civil time

Gregorian dates, clocks, appointments, legal deadlines, school terms, travel schedules, and public coordination.

Astronomical time

Observed lunar phase, solstices, equinoxes, sunrise, sunset, seasons, and measured celestial positions.

Codex time

A regular 13×28 witness matrix that gives daily records a repeating symbolic position inside the year.

These layers can be displayed together without claiming they are identical. The Codex calendar should show its symbolic Moon and Day coordinate alongside the Gregorian date and the separately calculated visible lunar phase.

Calendar Matrix

The 13×28 Form

$$ \boxed{ Y_{\mathrm{Codex}} = 13\times 28 = 364 } $$
$$ \boxed{ k = 28(m-1)+d } $$

A Codex year contains thirteen equal Moons. Each Moon contains 28 days. The coordinate \(m\) identifies the Moon, \(d\) identifies the Day, and \(k\) gives the position inside the 364-day matrix.

Transition and anchoring

A 364-day matrix does not equal the length of the tropical year. The system therefore requires a declared anchor and transition rule. The current Codex model begins from the first New Moon after the spring equinox and treats the threshold between matrices as an explicitly named transition period.

Cycle Architecture

Four weeks inside every Moon.

1–7 Opening

Define intention, frame the cycle, gather source, and begin the witness record.

8–14 Building

Act, create, compare, test, and observe what gains momentum.

15–21 Revelation

Review consequences, contradictions, repeating patterns, and hidden costs.

22–28 Return

Correct, complete, release, archive, and preserve the Remnant of the cycle.

These four phases are a Codex reading structure, not a claim that every person or event must follow the same emotional or biological pattern.

Field Layers

A daily reading can combine multiple visible layers.

Calendar layer

Moon, Day, week position, cycle phase, and year-map coordinate.

Astronomy layer

Visible lunar phase, season, daylight, and other separately calculated celestial information.

Witness layer

Dated events, observations, conditions, choices, and corrections.

Symbolic layer

Element, frequency label, sigil, breath pattern, archetype, and reading language.

Pattern layer

Repeated words, events, relationships, locations, numbers, or outcomes across prior logs.

Action layer

The concrete next step, boundary, repair, question, or practice chosen for the day.

Reading Language

A reading should reveal possibilities, not issue commands.

Mirror

Reflect the pattern the user has recorded and the stage of the active cycle.

Question

Ask a focused question that helps separate witness, memory, desire, and fear.

Threshold

Name what may be opening, closing, repeating, or requiring correction.

Action

Offer a grounded practice: record, speak, pause, repair, organize, build, rest, or release.

Boundary

Preserve choice and ethical responsibility. Symbolic language should not replace evidence or consent.

Remnant

End with what is worth carrying into the next day rather than endless interpretation.

Pattern Detection

Repetition becomes useful only when the frame is visible.

Record before alerting

A pattern should emerge from dated entries rather than from one vivid event.

Set the comparison window

State whether the pattern is daily, weekly, Moon-based, seasonal, or annual.

Count misses as well as matches

A trustworthy pattern review includes days when the expected event did not occur.

Separate symbol from cause

Two events sharing a number, phrase, or date may be meaningful to the witness without proving a causal mechanism.

Revise the pattern

When later entries weaken the interpretation, the alert should change rather than defend itself.

Ethical Calendar

Time systems can guide attention without governing another person.

No forced meaning

A reading should not assign motives, guilt, destiny, diagnosis, or obligation to someone who did not choose the framework.

No automatic authority

The calendar does not override law, medicine, safety, evidence, consent, or direct responsibility.

Visible calculation

Moon and Day coordinates, anchors, phase estimates, and saved records should be explainable and correctable.

Private by default

Personal witness logs should remain under the user’s control unless they choose to publish them.
