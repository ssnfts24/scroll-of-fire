Ethical Law

No field is coherent if it destroys the vessel carrying it.

Ethical Law is the containment layer of the Codex. It limits every equation, every practice, every claim, and every system. A method does not become acceptable merely because it is elegant, powerful, profitable, or internally consistent.

E9

Ethics is not the last page of the theory. It is the boundary around every page.

Orientation

Coherence is not the same as goodness.

Systems can be coherent and harmful

A structure may be efficient, repeatable, and internally consistent while violating dignity, hiding risk, concentrating control, or producing preventable harm.

Intention is not enough

Good intention does not erase harmful consequence. Ethical review compares purpose with action, impact, consent, and responsibility.

Correction is part of integrity

A living framework must be able to stop, revise, retract, repair, and visibly acknowledge when a claim or practice fails.

Equation 9 · Ethical Law

The Stop Rule

$$ \boxed{ \forall a: \operatorname{damage}(a)>0 \Rightarrow \mathsf{halt} } $$
$$ \boxed{ \lim_{t\to\infty} \frac{\operatorname{Truth}(t)} {\operatorname{Distortion}(t)} \uparrow } $$

When an action produces preventable harm, the operation must stop or change. Across time, the ratio of truth to distortion should increase rather than decrease.

How to read the stop rule

The symbol “damage” must be defined inside the frame. The Codex is not claiming that every discomfort or disagreement is harm. It requires proportional judgment, visible evidence, context, and attention to both immediate and delayed consequence.

Ethical Mask

The field must pass through containment.

$$ \mathfrak{R}_{\mathrm{allowed}} = \mathcal{E}_{9} \left[ \mathfrak{R}_{\mathrm{proposed}} \right] $$

The ethical mask \( \mathcal{E}_{9} \) reviews a proposed action or claim before it is treated as permitted within the Codex.

Pass

The action is proportionate, consensual where required, transparent, and responsibly contained.

Modify

The action may proceed only after risk, language, access, scope, or method is changed.

Hold

Evidence or consent is incomplete. The operation pauses until the missing conditions are resolved.

Halt

The action is harmful, deceptive, coercive, disproportionate, or incompatible with the declared boundary.

Core Laws

Eight ethical requirements.

1 Consent

Participation should be voluntary, informed, specific, and revocable where another person is involved.

2 Dignity

No person should be reduced to a symbol, resource, target, instrument, or obstacle.

3 Proportion

The scale of action should match the evidence, need, risk, and actual stakes.

4 Transparency

Purpose, method, uncertainty, source, funding, and conflict should be visible enough to examine.

5 Responsibility

The operator remains accountable for foreseeable consequence, not only declared intention.

6 Correction

False, overstated, outdated, or harmful claims must be revised where their effects can be reached.

7 Stewardship

Power, information, artifacts, records, land, tools, and access should be cared for rather than merely possessed.

8 Remnant

After correction, preserve only what remains useful, honest, proportionate, and worthy of return.

Consent

Permission must match the operation.

Informed

A person should understand what is being requested, what will happen, what will be recorded, and what risks are reasonably foreseeable.

Specific

Agreement to one use does not automatically authorize publication, resale, permanent storage, or a different experiment.

Revocable

Consent should be withdrawable where practical, especially before public release or irreversible use.

Uncoerced

Permission obtained through pressure, threat, dependency, deception, or withheld information is not full consent.

Truth / Distortion Ratio

The ethical direction of a living archive.

The Codex does not require instant certainty. It requires that uncertainty be named, evidence be separated from metaphor, errors become correctable, and the archive move toward greater clarity over time.

Label the layer

Mark material as observation, interpretation, theory, metaphor, tradition, testimony, or verified fact.

Preserve version history

Show what changed and why instead of silently overwriting earlier claims.

Name uncertainty

Possible, probable, unresolved, symbolic, and established are not interchangeable.

Correct at the same scale

A public error requires a public correction visible enough to reach the original audience.

Failure Modes

How ethical language can be used to hide unethical systems.

Virtue as shield

A declared noble purpose is used to block examination of method and consequence.

Consent theater

Permission is technically requested but the person lacks meaningful information, choice, power, or ability to refuse.

Selective transparency

Safe details are disclosed while motive, funding, source dependence, or known risk remains hidden.

Weaponized uncertainty

The absence of total certainty is used to delay correction despite strong evidence of harm.

False equivalence

Unequal evidence, risk, authority, or consequence is presented as though both sides carry the same weight.

Private correction, public claim

The error remains visible while the correction is hidden, limited, or difficult to find.

Practice

The Equation 9 audit.

Run this audit before action, publication, transfer, or deployment.

The audit can be used for a theory claim, artifact release, field test, public post, archive entry, software tool, family decision, or institutional process.

1 · Define the action

What exactly is being proposed, by whom, for whom, and for how long?

2 · Identify the affected

Who carries benefit, cost, exposure, risk, dependency, or loss of control?

3 · Verify consent

What permission exists, what does it cover, and can it be withdrawn?

4 · Map foreseeable harm

What physical, emotional, financial, legal, social, environmental, or reputational damage could follow?

5 · Test proportion

Is the action larger, more permanent, or more public than the evidence and need justify?

6 · Expose uncertainty

What is known, inferred, symbolic, assumed, or unresolved?

7 · Define the halt condition

What observable event would stop or change the operation?

8 · Assign responsibility

Who repairs damage, corrects the record, protects data, and carries the cost of failure?

9 · Preserve the decision

Record the reasoning, limits, consent, evidence, and review date.

Worked Example

Publishing a field-test claim.

Proposed claim

An artifact appeared to change a compass reading during one field test.

Evidence level

One observation under uncontrolled conditions with no independent replication.

Ethical risk

A strong performance claim could mislead buyers, followers, or people seeking health or safety outcomes.

Required modification

Publish the direct observation, conditions, uncertainty, and alternative explanations without claiming a confirmed mechanism.

Halt condition

Do not market the object as proven to generate a field unless repeatable measurements support that claim.

Remnant

The witnessed compass movement remains recordable; the causal conclusion remains unresolved.
