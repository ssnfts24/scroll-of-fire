Equation 0

Equation 0 names the pre-interpretive field of the Codex. It does not claim that every aspect of reality is already known or physically unified. It establishes the space in which source, witness, language, memory, coherence, causality, ethics, and Remnant can be located and compared.

Equation 0

The Foundational Field

$$ \boxed{ \Psi_{\Omega}(x,t) = \left\{ E,\, I,\, N,\, M,\, F,\, C \right\}_{\Omega,x,t} } $$

The Foundational Field \( \Psi_{\Omega}(x,t) \) is the declared set of active conditions—Energy, Information, Intention, Memory, Flow, and Consciousness— located inside a boundary \( \Omega \), position \(x\), and time \(t\).

Alternate compact form

$$ \Psi_{\Omega} = \operatorname{Frame} \left( E,I,N,M,F,C \right) $$

The alternate form emphasizes function over measurement: Equation 0 gathers the active terms into one declared field before any relation among them is calculated.

Plain Language

What Equation 0 means.

Nothing is observed from nowhere. Every inquiry begins inside a place, a time, a body, a language, a set of available materials, prior memories, active flows, and a boundary around what will count as relevant. Equation 0 requires those conditions to be named.

It begins before conclusion

The field is declared before deciding what an event means or which theory explains it.

It exposes the boundary

Every model excludes something. \( \Omega \) makes the chosen boundary visible.

It preserves location

The variables \(x\) and \(t\) prevent observations from being detached from place and time.

It supports revision

When the boundary, location, terms, or conditions change, the field definition must change with them.

Terms

Every symbol in Equation 0.

\( \Psi \)

Field state

The total declared configuration under examination. It may be conceptual, observational, social, material, acoustic, temporal, or multi-layered.

\( \Omega \)

Boundary

The scope defining what is inside, outside, relevant, excluded, permitted, measurable, or ethically constrained.

\( x \)

Position

The place, spatial relation, site, body position, object position, or other declared location coordinate.

\( t \)

Time

The instant, interval, sequence, Moon coordinate, civil date, session duration, or other declared temporal frame.

\( E \)

Energy

Available capacity for change: force, effort, heat, motion, charge, labor, attention, or stored potential.

\( I \)

Information

Pattern, measurement, instruction, language, code, geometry, signal, difference, and structure.

\( N \)

Intention

The declared direction, purpose, question, boundary, or desired action. \(N\) is used to avoid conflict with \(I\) for Information.

\( M \)

Memory

Prior witness, habit, archive, artifact history, retained structure, and weighted influence from earlier states.

\( F \)

Flow

Movement through the field: breath, water, signal, people, materials, money, attention, electricity, travel, or exchange.

\( C \)

Consciousness

The participating witness capable of attention, interpretation, choice, correction, and relationship.

Scope

What Equation 0 does and does not do.

It does

Define the field, declare the boundary, locate the inquiry, gather the active Master Equation terms, and prepare the system for witness.

It does not

Prove a universal physical field, replace established field equations, establish unseen causes, or make every included term measurable in one unit.

It can be operational

A practical project may define measurable proxies for each term and use Equation 0 as a structured field inventory.

It can be symbolic

A philosophical or artistic reading may use the relation to organize meaning, provided the symbolic layer is clearly labeled.

Field Declaration

Minimum information before analysis begins.

1 · Subject

What object, event, person, system, claim, artifact, or process is being examined?

2 · Boundary

What is included, excluded, private, public, measurable, symbolic, or unresolved?

3 · Place

Where is the field physically, socially, digitally, or relationally located?

4 · Time

What instant, duration, sequence, civil date, or Codex cycle defines the inquiry?

5 · Participants

Who acts, witnesses, controls, benefits, carries risk, or remains outside the frame?

6 · Active terms

What Energy, Information, Intention, Memory, Flow, and Consciousness are present?

7 · Evidence state

What is observed, documented, inferred, symbolic, hypothetical, or unresolved?

8 · Ethical boundary

What consent, privacy, safety, proportionality, and halt conditions apply?

Worked Example

Declaring a field test.

Subject

A compass reading observed near a copper, stone, ferrite, and magnet artifact.

Boundary \( \Omega \)

The artifact, compass, operator, room, nearby electronics, surface, orientation, and five-minute test interval.

Position \(x\)

The measured distance and angle between the compass and artifact.

Time \(t\)

Date, start time, duration, and sequence of baseline and active conditions.

Active terms

Energy: hand movement and magnetic interaction. Information: compass scale and notes. Intention: test for repeatable deflection. Memory: prior observation. Flow: movement through test positions. Consciousness: operator and observer.

Claim boundary

A compass movement may be recorded directly. A wider causal mechanism remains unresolved until controls and repeated measurement support it.

Failure Modes

How Equation 0 can be misused.

Boundary inflation

The field expands until every possible factor is included and no claim can be meaningfully tested.

Boundary concealment

Important participants, funding, equipment, prior knowledge, or environmental conditions remain outside the declared frame.

Symbolic-physical collapse

A meaningful symbolic relation is presented as though it were already a measured physical law.

Variable ambiguity

Terms such as Energy, Information, or Consciousness change meaning during the same argument.

Missing location

An observation is generalized beyond the place, equipment, person, or conditions in which it occurred.

Missing time

A momentary event is presented as permanent, recurring, or historically continuous without evidence.

Ethical Boundary

Equation 9 applies before the field is opened.

$$ \Psi_{\Omega}^{\,\mathrm{allowed}} = \mathcal{E}_{9} \left[ \Psi_{\Omega}^{\,\mathrm{proposed}} \right] $$

A field declaration must respect consent, privacy, safety, proportionality, responsibility, and visible halt conditions. A system cannot ethically include another person merely because the operator considers them relevant.
