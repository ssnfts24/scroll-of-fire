Equation 6

A field becomes Type-7 when its carriers are coordinated, witnessed, bounded, and revisable.

Equation 6 defines the Type-7 Field as a constructed working architecture. It combines selected carriers with gain, gates, phase, timing, routing, geometry, memory, witness, and Ethical Law. The equation does not establish a new accepted physical force; it provides a disciplined model for building, recording, testing, and revising coordinated systems.

Plain Language

What Equation 6 means.

A Type-7 system is not defined by one number, stone, coil, symbol, or tone. It is defined by how its parts are selected, connected, timed, observed, documented, corrected, and ethically used. The field exists as a working configuration, not as proof of every interpretation attached to it.

Carriers are selected

Tones, pulses, voice, light, movement, symbols, materials, or data enter for a declared reason.

Carriers are governed

Each layer has gain, timing, phase, routing, duration, and stop conditions.

The operator is recorded

Setup, intention, intervention, adjustments, and witness remain part of the field history.

The system remains revisable

Unsupported interpretations are narrowed while useful construction and observation are preserved.

Build Protocol

From intention to a witnessed Type-7 field.

1 · Declare the purpose

State what the field is meant to do, explore, display, support, or test.

2 · Define the boundary

Name the participants, place, duration, equipment, privacy level, and stop conditions.

3 · Select carriers

Choose each sound, visual, material, symbolic, operator, or data layer for a declared reason.

4 · Set gates

Define what is open, reduced, routed, delayed, muted, or excluded.

5 · Set gain and phase

Record intensity, relative timing, channel balance, orientation, and transition behavior.

6 · Map geometry and routing

Document component order, physical placement, signal path, input, output, and feedback routes.

7 · Establish baseline

Record the field before activation or compare against a simpler control configuration.

8 · Activate and witness

Record direct observations, measurements, operator changes, listener reports, and unexpected events.

9 · Apply correction

Reduce unsupported claims, isolate components, change gates, and repeat where useful.

10 · Preserve Remnant

Version the configuration and carry forward only the settings, observations, and meanings that survive review.

Scope

What Equation 6 does and does not do.

It does

Organize multi-carrier systems through explicit gates, gain, phase, timing, routing, witness, memory, and ethics.

It does not

Establish Type-7 as a recognized physical field or prove extraordinary effects from symbolic configuration alone.

It can be implemented

Audio, visual, software, installation, artifact, performance, and mixed-media systems can use the architecture directly.

It can be tested

Individual layers may be isolated, measured, repeated, compared, or removed to determine what changes.

Failure Modes

How a Type-7 field loses coherence.

Carrier accumulation

More layers are added without a declared purpose, making the field impossible to interpret or test.

Unrecorded gain

Volume, intensity, placement, or weight changes without entering the session record.

Symbolic-physical collapse

A meaningful number, material, name, or geometry is treated as proof of an unmeasured mechanism.

Missing baseline

A change is attributed to the field without recording the inactive or comparison condition.

Operator concealment

Manual adjustments, expectations, instructions, or intervention remain outside the record.

Ethical bypass

Technical possibility or symbolic purpose is treated as permission to expose others without consent.

Ethical Boundary

Equation 9 encloses the Type-7 field.

$$ \mathbb{T}_{7}^{\,\mathrm{allowed}} = \mathcal{E}_{9} \left[ \mathbb{T}_{7}^{\,\mathrm{proposed}} \right] $$

The field must remain voluntary, transparent, interruptible, proportionate, and technically safe. The operator’s symbolic interpretation does not override another participant’s consent or direct experience.

Consent

Participants should understand the carriers, purpose, duration, and ability to stop.

Level and exposure

Sound, light, heat, magnetism, electricity, movement, and physical contact must remain within safe use.

Transparency

Hidden speech, concealed edits, undisclosed components, and unrecorded operator intervention break the field contract.

Halt condition

Discomfort, unsafe behavior, equipment fault, loss of consent, or unknown risk closes the field.
