Comparison Frame

How an external model may enter Equation 7.

$$ \mathcal{K}_{7} = \operatorname{Compare} \left( \mathcal{X}_{\mathrm{ext}}, \mathcal{X}_{\mathrm{codex}} \right) $$

\( \mathcal{X}_{\mathrm{ext}} \) is the verified external model. \( \mathcal{X}_{\mathrm{codex}} \) is the Codex relation being compared. The comparator records overlap, difference, limits, and source status without treating resemblance as proof of identity.

Comparator role

Verification Protocol

From remembered name to reliable comparator.

1 · Preserve the remembered form

Record exactly how “Copeland” entered the project without expanding the memory into a claim.

2 · Search by domain

Identify the subject area originally associated with the comparator: physics, systems, economics, acoustics, cognition, or another field.

3 · Resolve the person

Distinguish among authors, researchers, inventors, or theorists who share the surname.

4 · Locate the primary source

Find the original publication or an authoritative archive rather than relying only on summaries.

5 · Capture the exact form

Preserve the equation, notation, surrounding explanation, units, and assumptions.

6 · Confirm independently

Use a second reliable source to verify attribution, interpretation, and publication details.

7 · Map the comparison

List structural overlap and explicit differences without forcing equivalence.

8 · Assign source status

Classify the source as primary, secondary, comparator, archive, or unresolved.

9 · Update connected pages

Revise the Canon, bibliography, glossary, notation, and any chapter citing Equation 7.

10 · Preserve the revision

Keep this unresolved edition in version history and document the date the comparator became verified.

Comparator Logic

Resemblance is not identity.

Structural similarity

Two models may both use feedback, weighting, fields, gradients, memory, or thresholds.

Different domain

Similar mathematics may describe entirely different systems, scales, or evidence bases.

Different units

Shared symbols do not make unlike quantities directly interchangeable.

Different purpose

One model may predict measurable behavior while another organizes interpretation or practice.

Independent convergence

Similar structure can emerge independently without proving copying, causation, or common origin.

Productive disagreement

A comparator may be most useful where it exposes a limit or contradiction in the Codex.

Failure Modes

How an external comparator can corrupt the theory.

Invented citation

A plausible source is presented as the intended source without evidence.

Name matching

A surname alone is treated as enough to identify the correct author.

Equation transplant

An external formula is removed from its original domain and assigned a new meaning without disclosure.

Similarity inflation

Shared vocabulary or visual resemblance is treated as proof of equivalence.

Authority borrowing

A respected external name is used to make an internal Codex claim appear established.

Silent replacement

The original unresolved comparator is swapped for a new one without preserving the revision history.
