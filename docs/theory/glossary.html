Theory Glossary

A shared language keeps the Codex connected without making every word mean everything.

This glossary defines the working vocabulary used across the Master Equation, Unified Field, Canon 0–17, 13 Moons, Frequency Governance, Living Technology, Artifact Registry, Witness Ledger, and related systems.

A made object carrying material, design, labor, name, provenance, witness, memory, current status, and stewardship.

Artifact Registry

The structured public or private record used to identify, classify, document, update, transfer, and archive Scroll of Fire artifacts.

Awareness

The situated capacity to notice internal or external conditions. In Equation 2, awareness is located in place and time rather than treated as abstract neutrality.

Canon

The numbered equation library of the Codex of Reality, presently organized as Equations 0–17 with Equation 4b as a companion language relation.

Carrier

A measurable or perceivable vehicle through which a signal or meaning is transmitted: tone, voice, rhythm, waveform, image, text, gesture, or material arrangement.

Causal Lattice

A structured map linking actions and conditions to consequences. It is used to prevent narrative from replacing sequence.

Equation 12 →

Claim Boundary

The visible distinction among material fact, intended function, direct witness, interpretation, symbolism, theory, and demonstrated performance.

Living Technology →

Coherence

The degree of fit among intention, language, source, witness, memory, behavior, evidence, consequence, and ethical boundary.

Equation 13 →

Consciousness

The participating witness capable of awareness, interpretation, choice, correction, and relationship. In the Master Equation, consciousness modifies how the other terms are organized and experienced.

Master Equation →

Codex Time

The symbolic 13×28 calendar coordinate used for recurring witness, memory review, daily reflection, and year mapping.

13 Moons Theory →

D

Delta Framework

The eight-stage sequence Δ0–Δ7: Frame, Source, Witness, Gate, Language, Memory, Coherence, and Remnant.

Δ0–Δ7 Framework →

Distortion

A mismatch introduced by error, omission, manipulation, memory change, hidden framing, unsupported certainty, or language that exceeds evidence.

Ethical Law →

E

Energy

Capacity for change, including motion, effort, heat, charge, metabolism, pressure, labor, stored potential, and available force.

Master Equation →

Ethical Law

Equation 9 and its supporting principles: consent, dignity, proportion, transparency, responsibility, correction, stewardship, and halt conditions.

Ethical Law →

Ethical Mask

The containment function \( \mathcal{E}_{9} \) that permits, modifies, holds, or halts a proposed Codex operation.

Ethical Mask →

Field

The declared system of conditions, participants, location, time, environment, variables, boundaries, and relationships under examination.

Unified Field →

Flow

Movement through a system: breath, water, electricity, circulation, communication, trade, travel, exchange, transition, and release.

Master Equation →

Frame

The declared scope of an inquiry: subject, time, location, participants, purpose, assumptions, witness position, and ethical boundary.

Δ0 · Frame →

Frequency Governance

The Codex framework for selecting, combining, naming, recording, witnessing, and ethically using sound carriers.

Frequency Governance →

G

Gate

A rule that permits, reduces, routes, filters, delays, or rejects a signal, claim, action, variable, participant, or carrier.

Equation 3 →

Gregorian Overlay

The civil calendar date displayed alongside the Codex Moon and Day coordinate. It preserves public chronology and coordination.

13 Moons Coordinates →

Equation 3, represented by \( \mathcal{H}(\nu) \), governing how strongly a frequency or carrier is allowed into a working field.

Equation 3 →

I

Information

Pattern and difference expressed through measurement, language, geometry, signal, instruction, code, symbol, story, or structure.

Master Equation →

Intention

Directed choice: attention given a purpose, target, promise, boundary, desired change, or declared action.

Living Operators →

Meaning assigned to an observation. Interpretation is useful but should remain distinguishable from direct witness and causal proof.

Living Operators

Technology treated as a relational system of material, design, operator, environment, witness, memory, maintenance, and ethical use.

Living Technology →

Master Equation →

Memory

Retained pattern carried through time in recollection, habit, skill, record, artifact, environment, institution, or inherited structure.

Memory →

What was directly seen, heard, measured, touched, recorded, or otherwise perceived before assigning explanation or cause.

Witness →

A transformation or declared action that changes the state, framing, direction, language, source, or coherence of a field.

Living Operators →

The relative position of a waveform inside its cycle. Phase relations affect reinforcement, cancellation, motion, and stereo perception.

Frequency Relations →

Phase-Locking

A declared coordination of timing or phase among carriers. In the Codex, this can describe audio design without proving an unmeasured external field.

Type-7 Field →

Projection Frame

Equation 11 →

Provenance

The traceable history of origin, making, materials, ownership, transfer, repair, condition, and stewardship attached to an artifact or record.

Artifact Provenance →

R

Reality

The resulting lived or modeled field represented by \( \mathfrak{R} \) in the Master Equation. The term is intentionally broad and must be framed before operational use.

Master Equation →

Remnant

What remains worth carrying forward after unsupported certainty, distortion, failed prediction, harmful action, and obsolete structure are removed.

Equation 5 →

Resonance

A relationship in which a system responds strongly to particular conditions, frequencies, patterns, meanings, or repeated structures. Physical and symbolic uses should be labeled separately.

Frequency Governance →

Equation 4b →

The actual supply feeding a system: material, energy, information, ancestry, labor, funding, instruction, environment, dependency, and constraint.

Living Operators →

The current state of an artifact or project, such as Personal Instrument, Available, Reserved, Gifted, In Creation, Coming Soon, Archived, or Retired.

Artifact Lifecycle →

Stewardship

Responsible care for an artifact, record, material, place, system, or body of knowledge through use, maintenance, transfer, correction, and release.

Artifact Stewardship →

The mythic, spiritual, artistic, personal, or archetypal meaning assigned to a number, object, phrase, sound, time coordinate, or practice.

Revision Rule

Definitions must retain their history.
