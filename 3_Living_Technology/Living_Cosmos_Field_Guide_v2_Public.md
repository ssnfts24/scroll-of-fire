# The Living Cosmos Field Guide
## A step-by-step manual anyone can use — no science background needed

**Version:** 2.0 – Public Edition
**Date:** 2026-05-05

---

### Who this is for

- You are curious about how attention and calm might affect your day
- You do not have a physics degree
- You want clear instructions, not beliefs

If that is you, you can use this.

### What you will need (all optional, start with just a notebook)

1. A notebook or phone notes app
2. A timer (phone timer works)
3. Optional: free HRV app (search "HRV" in app store, pick one with a heart icon)
4. Optional: a quiet place to sit for 3 minutes

You do not need the T7 device to start. The device is a later tool, like adding an amplifier to a guitar you already know how to play.

### The big picture in plain words

Imagine five nested musical instruments:

1. **You** – a small hand drum
2. **A simple resonator** (the T7 later) – a wooden box that makes the drum louder
3. **Earth** – a giant bass drum that hums at about 8 beats per second
4. **Many people together** – an orchestra
5. **The galaxy** – the concert hall itself

This guide teaches you to play your small drum steadily. Everything else is context.

### The four skills (you already use these, we just name them)

**1. POINT – Choose a direction**
Like aiming a flashlight. Vague is "I want things better." Clear is "I want to finish my passport application this week."

**2. PATTERN – Give it a shape**
Use a short sentence, 4 to 7 words. Say the most important word a little slower. Example: "I choose calm action now." The word "calm" gets the weight.

**3. STEADINESS – Get quiet inside**
Check three body signals:
- Jaw: is it clenched? Let it drop a millimeter
- Shoulders: are they up? Let them fall
- Breath: can you make the out-breath longer than the in-breath? Count in for 4, out for 6

If you can do those three, you are steady enough.

**4. STOP IF HARM – The safety switch**
Ask three quick questions:
- Does this hurt my body?
- Does this break a promise I made?
- Does this trick or force someone?
If yes to any, stop and pick a different point.

### Daily practice – the 5-minute loop (do this exactly)

Do this 3 times a day: morning, midday, evening.

**Minute 1 – Settle**
Sit, feet flat. Timer 60 seconds. Do nothing but notice your out-breath. Count 4 in, 6 out, 5 times.

**Minute 2 – Point and Pattern**
Say your sentence silently once. Example: "I choose calm action now." Feel the weighted word.

**Minute 3 – Check Steadiness**
Ask: jaw, shoulders, breath. Adjust if needed. If still tense after 30 seconds, that is okay, just note "tense."

**Minute 4 – One small action**
Do the smallest physical step toward your point. Not thinking about it — doing it. Examples:
- Point = passport → action = open the government website
- Point = less arguing → action = send one kind text
- Point = more money → action = write down one expense to cut

**Minute 5 – Log**
Write 4 things:
1. Time
2. Steadiness score 1-10 (10 = very calm)
3. Action taken (one verb)
4. What happened next in one sentence

That is it. No visualization, no chanting, no belief required.

---

### Weekly check – what to look for

After 7 days, open your notebook. Make three columns on a blank page:

| Day | Steadiness 7 or higher? | Useful thing happened within 2 hours? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mon | yes/no | yes/no |
| Tue |  |  |

Count the yes-yes matches. If you have 3 or more in a week, your steadiness is correlating with useful outcomes for you. That is your personal data.

---

### If you have a phone HRV app

1. Open app, sit still 1 minute, get baseline number
2. Do Minute 1 breathing
3. Check number again
You are looking for the number to go up by even 2-5 points. Write it down. Over weeks, you will see a pattern.

---

### If you later use a T7 or similar device

Do not change the practice. Just place your hands near the device during Minute 2 and 3. The device does not replace steadiness, it only holds the tone a little longer, like a sustain pedal on a piano. If you feel agitated, turn it off. It amplifies whatever you bring.

### Troubleshooting

**"I cannot get steady"**
- Shorten to 2 breaths instead of 5. Log "2 breaths, score 3." That counts.
- Try standing instead of sitting.

**"Nothing happens"**
- Good data. Write "nothing." After 10 "nothings," change your point to something smaller and more physical.

**"I feel weird using a device"**
- Do not use it. The practice works without it. The device is optional.

**"This sounds like religion"**
- It is not. No belief is required. You are measuring breath and actions, not praying to anything.

---

### What to ignore completely while learning

1. Arguments about flat earth, round earth, dimensions, aliens. They do not change your breath.
2. Anyone selling "quantum" products that promise results without practice.
3. Trying to affect other people directly. Work only on your own point and action.
4. Big words in the technical papers until you have 30 days of logs.
